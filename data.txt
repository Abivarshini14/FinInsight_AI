The 50/30/20 rule is a simple budgeting method that helps people manage their money effectively. It suggests spending 50% of your income on needs, 30% on wants, and allocating 20% to savings and debt repayment.

An Emergency Fund is a stash of money set aside to cover financial surprises like medical emergencies or car repairs. Most financial experts recommend having 3 to 6 months' worth of living expenses saved in a liquid account.

A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) is a method of investing a fixed sum of money regularly in a mutual fund scheme. SIPs allow you to buy more units when the price is low and fewer units when the price is high, helping with rupee cost averaging.

Diversification is a risk management strategy that mixes a wide variety of investments within a portfolio. The rationale is that a portfolio constructed of different kinds of assets will, on average, yield higher long-term returns and lower the risk of any individual holding.
